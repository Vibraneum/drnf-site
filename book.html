The Beginning of Punjabi Nationalism

A Heritage Book by Rai Bahadur Mul Raj (1855-1945)

Autobiography of Dr. Rabinder Nath's Great-Great-Grandfather - Founder of Punjab National Bank, Arya Samaj Leader, and Pioneer of Indian Nationalism

Why Read This Book?

A firsthand account of India's transformation through the eyes of a pioneer who shaped Punjab's destiny

Birth of Punjab Nationalism

Witness the origins of regional consciousness and identity politics that shaped modern India. Mul Raj's firsthand accounts reveal how Punjab transformed from a colonial territory into a cultural powerhouse.

Institutional Legacy

Discover how one man founded institutions that still serve millions - Punjab National Bank (India's 2nd largest bank), D.A.V. College (900+ institutions), and the Arya Samaj reform movement.

Preserving Heritage

This autobiography was nearly lost to history. Written in the 1930s, published in 1975, and relaunched in 2016 - it's a rare window into Punjab's golden era through the eyes of someone who lived it.

Unbiased Historical Account

Unlike many politically motivated histories, this is a personal narrative dictated to family. Scholars consider it essential primary source material for reconstructing the true history of the Arya Samaj and Punjab's freedom movement.

About the Book

"The Beginning of Punjabi Nationalism" is a remarkable book written by Dr. Upender Nath about the life and times of Rai Bahadur Mul Raj, a towering figure in India's freedom movement and social reform.

Originally dictated in the 1930s to his grandson Dr. Upender Nath (Royal Surgeon to the Nizam of Hyderabad), this extraordinary document captures firsthand accounts of pivotal moments in Indian history.
